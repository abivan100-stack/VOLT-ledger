04Trade-offs

Why not just a database?

Because a database would work — if everyone agreed to trust whoever runs it. Volt's bet is narrower: neighbors settling money with each other shouldn't have to.

A TRUSTED DATABASETHE VOLT LEDGER
WRITES One operator writes. Fast, cheap, final. Any household writes; every other household verifies.
TAMPERING Silent. An edited row looks like any other row. Loud. One changed digit voids every entry after it — you just saw it.
COST Near zero. This column wins on price, honestly. Real overhead — hashing and replication aren't free.
DISPUTES Escalate to the operator, and hope. Replay the chain. The math settles it.
VERDICT Right when there's a utility everyone already trusts. Right when the operator is the thing in question.
